Book 32: KITAB AL-BIRR WA'S-SALAT-I-WA'L-ADAB
(The book of virtue, good manners and joining of the ties of relationship)

Chapter
  1. Politeness towards parents and their right to it
  2. The preference of benevolent treatment to parents over voluntary prayers, etc.
  3. Kindness towards the friends of one's father and mother
  4. Joining the tie of, relationship and prohibition to break it
  5. Forbiddance of nursing mutual jealousy mutual hatred and mutual hostility
  6. It is forbidden for a Muslim to have estranged relations with the other Muslim beyond three days without any reason of Shari'ah
  7. Forbiddance of suspicion, fault-finding, and bidding against the other, (for raising the price)
  8. It is forbidden to pwpbuate atrocity upon a Muslim, to humiliate him, to insult him, and inviolable is his blood, honour and wealth
  9. It is forbidden to nurse rancour
  10. Merit of love for the sake of Allah
  11. Merit of visiting the sick
  12. Whenever a believer falls sick or is stricken with grief or something tike it, there is a reward for him even if it is pricking of a thorn
  13. It is forbidden to commit oppression
  14. Help your brother whether he is an oppressor or an oppressed one
  15. There should be mutual fellow-feeling and love and the will to help each other amongst the believers
  16. It is forbidden to indulge in abusing
  17. The meritq of forgiveness and humility
  18. The prohibition of backbiting
  19. Tidings for one whose faults Allah concealed in this world: He will also conceal his faults in the hereafter
  20. According of benevolent treatment to one from whom one expects transgression
  21. Merit of benevolent treatment
  22. It is forbidden to curse the beasts
  23. He upon whom Allah's Apostle (may peace be upon him) invoked curse whereas he in fact did not deserve it, it would be a source of reward and mercy for him
  24. The condemnation of one who plays a double game and the prohibition of this behaviour
  25. Forbiddance of telling a lie and the cases in which telling of lie is permissible
  26. Serious prohibition of tale-carrying
  27. The evil of a lie and the goodiess of truth and its merit
  28. The merit of self control at the time of anger
  29. It is the very nature of man that he loses control
  30. It is forbidden to strike at the face
  31. Grim warning to one who torments people without any valid justification
  32. He who goes in the mosque or in the bazar or a place of gathering like it with a weapon should see that the spearhead does not harm anyone
  33. The prohibition of pointing a weapon towards a Muslim
  34. The merit of removing of anything troublesome from the path
  35. Forbiddance of tormenting the cat or the animals like that which do not harm
  36. The prohibition of pride and vanity
  37. It is forbidden to become frustrated in regard to the mercy of Allah
  38. The merits of the weak and downtrodden people
  39. It is forbidden to use the expression: "The people are undone".
  40. Benevolent treatment towards the neighbour
  41. Excellence of meeting one with a cheerful countenance
  42. The merit of recommendation which involves nothing unlawful
  43. The merit of good company and avoidance from evil company
  44. The merit of according benevolent treatment to daughters
  45. The merit of one who resigns himself to the will of God calmly at the time of the death of one's son
  46. When Allah loves a servant, his fellow-servants also begin to love him
  47. Souls are troops collected together
  48. A person would be along with him whom he loves
  49. When a pious person is praised that is glad tidings for him
